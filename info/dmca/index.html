1. Respect for Intellectual Property

BSR Studios and AnyVidWall respect the intellectual property rights of others. It is our policy to respond to valid claims of copyright infringement in accordance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and other applicable intellectual property laws.

Please note that AnyVidWall is a local software utility and does not host, distribute, or upload user media files to our servers. However, if you believe that any content hosted on our official website infringes upon your copyright, you may submit a takedown notice.

2. Submitting a Takedown Notice

If you are a copyright owner or an agent acting on their behalf, and you believe that content on our website infringes your copyrights, please send a written notification to our Designated Copyright Agent with the following information:

  • A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized representative.
  • Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed.
  • Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing, along with the exact URL or location on our website where it can be found.
  • Your contact information, including your name, address, telephone number, and email address.
  • A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  • A statement made under penalty of perjury that the information in the notification is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

3. Counter-Notification

If you believe that content you uploaded or provided to us was removed by mistake or misidentification, you may send a counter-notice. Your counter-notice must include:

  • Your physical or electronic signature.
  • Identification of the material that has been removed and the location at which the material appeared before it was removed.
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  • Your name, address, telephone number, and a statement consenting to the jurisdiction of your local federal court.
